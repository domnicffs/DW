How to Play
DONKEY WHO — FULL RULES
🫏 THE GOAL
Be one of the first to empty your hand. The last player still holding cards when everyone else has finished is THE DONKEY and loses. Everyone else wins — 1st out wins the most!
🎴 SETUP
👥
4 players. Each player gets 13 playing cards and 1 special card. The remaining 12 special cards go in a pile in the middle.
🃏
Playing cards come in 4 colours — Blue, Red, Green, Purple — numbered 1 to 13.
💙
The player holding Blue 13 always goes first.
🔄 HOW A ROUND WORKS
1️⃣
The starting player plays any card face up. That card's colour becomes the round colour — for example Blue.
2️⃣
Going clockwise, every other player must play a card of the same colour. You can play any number — high or low, your choice.
3️⃣
Once every player has put a card down, all cards are discarded — nobody picks them up. The player who played the highest number that round must start the next round.
⚠️ Starting a round is a disadvantage! You go first blind — you don't know what anyone else will play. Try to avoid ending up with the highest card.
💥 THE DONK
😱
If you have NO cards in the round colour, you cannot play normally. Instead you must DONK — play any card from your hand on top of the pile.
🎯
When a DONK happens, whoever played the highest card in that round picks up ALL cards on the table — including the DONK card. Their hand gets much bigger!
🛑
Only ONE DONK per round. The round ends the moment someone DONKs. The player who got DONKed starts the next round.
🧠
Remember who DONKs! If someone DONKs on Blue, it means they have zero Blue cards. Every time you play Blue in future, they'll have to DONK again. Use this to plan your attacks!
💡 Smart play: Try to get rid of colours you have only 1 or 2 of early. Once those are gone, you can freely DONK when that colour comes up — sending the problem to someone else!
🚪 FINISHING & LEAVING
🎉
If you play your last card in a round and the round ends without you getting DONKed — you leave the game! You're a winner 🏆. The game continues without you.
😬
BUT — if you play your last card and someone DONKs you in that same round, you must pick up all those cards and keep playing. You can't escape a DONK!
🏁
When a player finishes, the remaining players carry on clockwise. The last person left is The Donkey 🫏.
✨ SPECIAL CARDS — OVERVIEW
✨
Every player holds 1 special card at a time, separate from their playing cards. After you use yours, you draw a replacement from the pile at the end of the round — in the order players used them that round. Once the pile runs out, no more replacements. There are only 16 special cards total so use them wisely!
👁️
You can see whether your opponents have or don't have a special card, but you cannot see which one they're holding — that's secret until played!
🔮 JACKASS JINX (JJ)
⏰
When: Only when you have just been DONKed.
⚡
What it does: Reverses the DONK back to the person who DONKed you. Now they have to pick up all the cards instead of you!
🔄
Can they counter? Yes — if the DONKer also has a Jackass Jinx, they can play it straight back at you. But that's it — the chain stops there. If it comes back to you a second time, you pick up the cards.
🛑
Rule: The JJ chain can only go back and forth once each. No more than two JJs in a row.
🛑 HOOF HALT (HH)
⏰
When: Only when you have just been DONKed — including after a Jackass Jinx has bounced the DONK to you.
⚡
What it does: Completely cancels the DONK. All cards on the table go to the discard pile — nobody picks them up.
⚠️
The trade-off: The player who used Hoof Halt must start the next round. That's the price for cancelling the DONK.
💡
Special case: If the Hoof Halt player has no cards left after cancelling, they're finished and the next active player clockwise from them starts the round instead.
🔄 SADDLE SWITCH (SS)
⏰
When: Only at the very start of a round, before any playing cards have been put down.
⚡
What it does: You choose another player to start the round instead of you. They must now go first.
🔄
Can they counter? Yes — if they also have a Saddle Switch, they can redirect it to anyone they choose, including back to you. But the chain stops after one exchange — if it comes back to you, you start the round.
🛑
Important rule: A Saddle Switch CANNOT be played after a Bray Break has been used to start the round. Once someone has played a Bray Break, it's too late for a Saddle Switch.
⏭️ BRAY BREAK (BB)
⏰
When: At any point during a round — whether you're starting it, in the middle, or near the end.
⚡
What it does: You skip your turn completely. You don't have to play a card this round at all.
🔗
Can others chain it? Yes! Multiple players can play Bray Break in the same round. If Player 1, 2 and 3 all Bray Break, only Player 4 plays a card — and that card is safely discarded with no risk.
🛑
Rules:
• You cannot Bray Break if you would be the last person left to play — someone must play a card.
• A Saddle Switch cannot be played after a Bray Break has started the round.
• A Bray Break can be played after a Saddle Switch chain has finished.
🌟 QUICK STRATEGY GUIDE
🔥
Play high cards early. In the first few rounds, play your 10s, 11s, 12s and 13s. High cards force you to start rounds later when it's much riskier.
🎨
Clear out your weak colours. If you only have 1 or 2 cards of a colour, lead with that colour or play those cards when you can. Once they're gone, you can freely DONK on that colour.
👀
Track the DONKs. Every DONK tells you something. Write it in your head — Player 2 has no Red, Player 3 has no Green. Use that to target them!
🔮
Save JJ and HH. These are your lifesavers. Don't waste them — wait until someone DONKs you with a big pile of cards, then strike!
🔄
Use SS and BB to cycle. If you have a Saddle Switch or Bray Break, you can use them just to get a new special card from the pile — hoping to get a JJ or HH for later.
🫏 One last thing: When someone gets DONKed and picks up cards — those are the cards played that round, including the DONK card. It can be a lot! That's why avoiding a DONK is so important.
